Our Professors

Algonquin College provides the Interactive Media Design program with highly intellectual professors that are intended to professionally educate students to gain skills needed in the industry. All of the professors have an education in the field as well as multiple years of experience within the business world; this allows for better teaching abilities. Interactive Media Design professors are expected to follow the overall course material provided, as well as contribute any additional information they seem fit for the class. Faculty members are foreseen to instruct, lecture and educate students at the highest capability. Although this is what is expected, some professors offer more than what is expected; helping students beyond class and office hours and giving further guidance to provide a positive outcome in the potential future of the pupil. The instructors of Algonquin College in this field are highly respected for their excellent application of knowledge for forthcoming graduates.

McKenney teaches the web production for the third term, and web applications for the fourth term in the Interactive Media Design program at Algonquin College. HUSSIEN TAYRANI

Video Foundations

Hussein Tayrani is a professor at Algonquin College in the School of Media and Design and a Creative Lead at Wilden Media. He teaches Design, Video and Motion Graphics courses for IMD. Helping his students to thrive and become professionals at work are among his main goals. Hussein's motivation is powered by a special fuel he identifies as his 'Coffee-tivity', the combination of coffee with creativity. When his colleagues see him tackling a project with a cup of coffee in his hand, there is no doubt he is up to something genius. On a regular day, if not teaching or working on Motion Graphics projects, Hussein is watching, playing, or reading news about soccer.

JOHN KOZODOJ


Applied Projects

John Kozodoj is one of the main professors for the Applied Projects course in the Interactive Media Design program at Algonquin College. He believes in letting students experiment with their creative abilities within the industry to give them the overall experience of working in the field. Doing so, John also leads students with his expertise offering them beneficial advice to improve their current skills, and to gain new ones. His teaching techniques gives students the wisdom on how to approach potential future clients in a professional manner, while implementing their own artistry in the employment field. Mr. Kozodoj makes students graduate the program feeling accomplished and ready to work in the area of media and design. Having giving them constructive criticism through the process of completing the projects in the course.
